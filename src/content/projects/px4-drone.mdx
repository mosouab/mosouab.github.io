GPS is great until you go indoors, under a bridge, or anywhere it matters. This
project is a complete autonomy stack for a quadrotor that keeps flying when GPS
doesn't: visual-inertial odometry, 3D mapping, and precision landing — all running
on a Raspberry Pi 4 bolted to the airframe.

## The stack

Five layers, from motors to mission:

1. **PX4 (Pixhawk)** — low-level stabilization and flight control, in SITL or on the real airframe.
2. **ROS 2 Jazzy** — orchestrates every perception and navigation node.
3. **MAVROS** — the bridge between MAVLink and ROS 2 topics/services.
4. **VIO + RTAB-Map** — local pose estimation with loop-closure drift correction, fed by an Intel RealSense D455 depth camera and fused into PX4's EKF2.
5. **Precision landing** — ArUco marker detection (OpenCV), velocity servoing toward the target, then an automatic `AUTO.LAND` trigger.

The mission layer exists twice on purpose: once in **C against raw MAVLink** (to
understand what's actually on the wire) and once in **Python with MAVSDK** (to get
things done). Waypoint missions, return-to-launch, takeoff — all validated in Gazebo
SITL before touching real propellers.

## The hardware

Holybro X500 V2 frame, Pixhawk flight controller, Raspberry Pi 4 as companion
computer, Intel RealSense D455 for depth and tracking. The whole software stack is
containerized — one `docker compose up` brings up PX4 SITL, Gazebo, MAVROS, RTAB-Map,
and the camera bridges in a tmux session, on Ubuntu or WSL2.

## What I learned the hard way

Getting RTAB-Map odometry trusted by EKF2 is 10% configuration and 90% reading
PX4 documentation at 2am. CPU budget on a Pi 4 is real: the perception pipeline had
to be profiled and trimmed until SLAM, marker detection, and MAVROS could share four
cores without starving each other. And a drone that lands itself on a 20 cm marker
is deeply satisfying to watch.
