I finally put together a personal site. Here's what it is and why I made it.

I'm a Master's student in automatic control and intelligent systems at Paris-Saclay.
I work on things that move, like drones, and things that shouldn't stop moving, like
factory equipment. I wanted a single link that covers all of it: something a recruiter
can open from a QR code, and something another engineer can actually read into.

The site runs on [Astro](https://astro.build) with plain CSS. No framework, no
tracking, just markdown files and fast HTML. The design is borrowed from
[stormix.dev](https://stormix.dev) and flipped to a light theme.

When I have something worth sharing, I'll write it here: notes on ROS 2 and PX4,
running neural networks on hardware that probably shouldn't run them, and the odd
opinion about PLCs. No fixed schedule.

If that sounds like your kind of thing, [the projects](/#projects) are a good place
to start.
