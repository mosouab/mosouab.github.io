Every engineer eventually builds a personal website instead of doing something
useful. This is mine.

The short version: I'm a Master's student in automatic control & intelligent systems
at Paris-Saclay, I like machines that move (drones) and machines that shouldn't stop
moving (factory equipment), and I needed one link that explains all of that —
something a recruiter can scan from a QR code and a fellow engineer can dig into.

The site itself is [Astro](https://astro.build) with plain CSS — no framework, no
tracking, just markdown files and fast HTML. The design is openly borrowed from
[stormix.dev](https://stormix.dev), flipped from dark to light. Imitation,
flattery, etc.

What I'll write about here, when something is worth writing about: ROS 2 and PX4
war stories, running neural networks on hardware that really shouldn't run neural
networks, and the occasional PLC opinion. No schedule, no newsletter, no hustle.

If any of that sounds like your kind of thing — [the projects](/#projects) are the
best place to start.
